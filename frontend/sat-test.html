📚 Digital SAT Overview

Important information for students new to the SAT

🎯 What is the Digital SAT?

The SAT (Scholastic Assessment Test) is a standardized test widely used for college admissions in the United States. The digital SAT replaced the paper test in 2024 and features adaptive testing technology.

💡 Test-Taking Strategies

⏰

Manage Your Time

Keep track of time. Don't spend too long on any single question. Flag difficult questions and return to them later.

✓

Answer Every Question

No guessing penalty! If you're running out of time, make your best guess on remaining questions.

🚩

Use the Flag Feature

Flag questions you want to review. Use the review screen before submitting each module.

📖

Read Carefully

Read questions and passages carefully. Pay attention to words like "NOT," "EXCEPT," and "MOST."

✏️

Process of Elimination

Eliminate obviously wrong answers first. This improves your chances when guessing.

🎯

Stay Focused

Take a deep breath between modules. Stay confident and focused throughout the test.

🔢 Math Section Details

Heart of Algebra (30%)

Linear equations, inequalities, systems of equations

Problem-Solving & Data Analysis (24%)

Ratios, percentages, data interpretation, probability

Passport to Advanced Math (30%)

Quadratic equations, exponential functions, polynomials

Additional Topics (16%)

Geometry, trigonometry, complex numbers

Calculator Policy: You can use a calculator for ALL math questions in this practice test. On the real SAT, a built-in Desmos calculator is available for the entire Math section.

📖 Reading & Writing Details

Information and Ideas (30%)

Central ideas, command of evidence, inferences

Craft and Structure (30%)

Words in context, text structure, purpose

Expression of Ideas (20%)

Rhetorical synthesis, transitions

Standard English Conventions (20%)

Grammar, sentence structure, punctuation

⚡ Quick Tips Before You Start

  • Find a quiet place free from distractions
  • Have scratch paper and pencil ready (for math)
  • Ensure you have a stable internet connection
  • Plan for 2+ hours if taking the full test
  • Don't rush - accuracy is more important than speed
  • Review your answers before submitting each module
  • Stay calm and do your best!
